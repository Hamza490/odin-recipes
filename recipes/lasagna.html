Recipe: Lasagna

Where did Lasagna originate?

Lasagna first made is a grand entrance in Naples, Italy during the Middle Ages in the 14th century. Initially, this dish was reserved for special events and holidays. Ironically, this pleasure inducing food was introduced to Italians in the middle of the Black Plague.

Ingredients

  • 8 oz Prince Lasagna noodles
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 2 cups pasta sauce
  • 1 3/4 cups ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 2 tablespoons grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 2 teaspoons dried parsely flakes
  • Salt and ground black pepper, to taste

Steps

Preparing the Pasta

  1. Cook the Prince Lasagna noodles according to the package instructions.
  2. Once cooked, lay the noodles flat on foil to cool, preventing them from sticking together.

Layering Techniques

  1. In a skillet, cook the ground beef until brown, then drain and stir in the pasta sauce.
  2. In a separate bowl, mix ricotta, one cup of mozzarella, Parmesan cheese, the egg, and seasoning.
  3. In a 13x9x2-inch baking dish, start by spreading a layer of meat sauce.
  4. Place three pasta pieces lengthwise over the sauce.
  5. Spread half of the ricotta mixture over the pasta, then cover with a cup of meat sauce.
  6. Repeat these layers, finishing with a layer of pasta and meat sauce.
  7. Top with the remaining mozzarella cheese.

Baking Tips

  1. Preheat your oven to 350 F.
  2. Cover the dish with foil and bake for 30 minutes.
  3. Remove the foil and bake for another 15 minutes or until the lasagna is hot and bubbly.
  4. Let it stand for 10 minutes before serving.
